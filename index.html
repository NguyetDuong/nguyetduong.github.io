Who is the Boba Queen?

Name: Nguyet Duong
Nickname: Mimi
Age: 19
School: University of California, Berkeley
Profession: Full time boba drinker

If you look on the left, you will see me! But don't expect too much out of me when you see me in person. I normally don't look that good -- I swear it's all the photographer, lightroom, photoshop, and camera. But don't worry, it didn't go through such a massive edit to the point you won't be able to recognize me on the streets. I hope from this paragraph you can tell I have a weird sense of humor. Of course, you're probably still wondering who I am.


I was born in Vietnam and came to America when I was young. At the time, I spent most of my days playing with dolls and stuffed animals like many kids do. It wasn't until sometime in elementary school that we were forced to take a computer class. Honestly, I didn't think much of it at the time... it was more of playing the typing games that made me so happy. Let's just say I rocked those typing games. And it is also the reason why I type decently fast today.


Then come the age of video games online. I'm specifically talking about Neopets. Most of you probably know what it is. However, I specifically remember that in order to play Neopets, I needed to sign up. And in order to sign up, I needed an email address. But I was eight? nine? at the time, there's no way I could have known how to create an email address.


Luckily, the computer teacher said she can help me make one if I bring in a note with my parent's permission on it. And that was the start of my love for technology. It was all a domino affect and I ended up playing on multiple different websites, different games, and met many different people from all around the world. This chain of actions led me to where I am today and made me understand what I am passionate about.


Wow, that was sort of cheesy. But aside from the computer and all of this jazz, I spend a lot of my time hanging out at boba (otherwise known as tapioca drink, milk tea) shops. If you are from California, it is a big hype right now. And I'm not even going to lie. I am addicted to this stuff. So if you run into me on the streets, there is a 95% chance that I will have some sort of boba cup on me.
